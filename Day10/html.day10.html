Second : what's the problem

so like we said before we are humens

and as a humens it happens and we go through tough situation through our life

some of this situation left a mark on us and we name it as trauma

our brain remembers the experiences that hurt us the most

after enough of these experiences it starts creating ways to protect us from feeling that pain again

sometimes those ways help us

sometimes they end up making relationships harder

attachment issues usually develop during childhood through repeated experiences with caregivers. Trauma can contribute to them, but inconsistent care, emotional neglect, or unpredictable relationships can also shape attachment patterns

so it builds a defence mechanism against it

and that what i found in my research

some of these protective patterns affect how we communicate, trust people, and respond emotionally in relationships.

and they name it as attachment styles

It is divided into four parts

  1. Anxious Attachment
  2. Avoidant Attachment
  3. Disorganized Attachment
  4. Secure Attachment

let's explain each one on it's own

First: Anxious Attachment

people who been through alot of life situations when they being abandoned in

their brains develop this mechanism

so when they meet someone they care about and finds out that someone care about them too

they feel overwhelmed

and when things become cold in the relationship their fear gets triggered

their nervous system becomes highly activated

they start over thinking every single thing , they start thinking are they important to that person or not , they just start thinking they are being abandoned again

they panic , feel chest tightness , rapid heartbeat and heavy breathing

so they react with these feeling with doubletexting , agrassive texting and over apologizing

so they try to get answers to go over this intense anxiety or panic-like feelings.

their behavior usually comes from fear of losing someone important to them

their brain isn't trying to make them suffer. It's trying to protect them from experiencing abandonment again. The problem is that it sometimes reacts even when there isn't actual danger.

What their brain slowly starts believing

"if i don't fight for people, they will leave me."

"i'm not enough."

"love can disappear at any moment."

we can say it in one quote

"If people leave me, I'll be alone"

Second: Avoidant Attachment

this attachment style is one of the most misunderstood ones

many people think these people don't care about others or don't want relationships

but that's usually not true

most of them actually want love, care and relationships just like everyone else

the difference is what happens when they start getting close to someone

their brain starts feeling pressure

the closer they get, the more uncomfortable they feel

not because they hate that person

not because they stopped caring

but because their brain learned that getting attached to someone can end up hurting them

so it creates its own defence mechanism

instead of running toward people when they are struggling

they run away and try to deal with everything alone

they start thinking

"i need some space"

"i can handle this alone"

"i don't want to be a burden"

"it's easier if i just disappear for a while"

so they start pulling away

they reply less

they talk less

they spend more time alone

sometimes they disappear for days

sometimes even weeks

to the other person it looks like they stopped caring

but inside they are usually trying to calm their nervous system down

the problem is that they don't always explain what is happening

because they find it hard to talk about their feelings

they feel pressured doing it

and sometimes they don't even understand what they are feeling themselves

so they stay silent instead

sometimes they think disappearing for a while will protect both themselves and the other person

they don't always realize that the silence can hurt the other person even more than the conversation they are trying to avoid

they usually live in a loop

they meet someone

they enjoy talking to them

they get closer

their feelings grow

their brain goes back into protection mode

they pull away

they calm down

they start missing that person

they come back

they get close again

then they goes back to the protection mode again

and the cycle repeats

their behavior is usually coming from fear of getting hurt and fear of emotional pressure

What their brain slowly starts believing

"if i depend on someone, i'll get hurt."

"it's safer to handle everything alone."

"showing my feelings will only make things harder."

we can say it in one quote

"i want connection, but i'm scared of what comes with it"

Third : Disorganized Attachment

this one is considered the most confusing attachment style

because it is a mix between anxious attachment and avoidant attachment

they deeply want love, care and emotional closeness

but at the exact same time they become scared when someone gets too close

their brain sends them two opposite messages

"don't leave them"

and at the same time

"run before they hurt you"

so they often live in a constant internal conflict

sometimes they become very affectionate and attached

then suddenly pull away without understanding why

they may overthink like someone with anxious attachment

but react by distancing themselves like someone with avoidant attachment

this creates a push-and-pull cycle in relationships

they may want reassurance, then reject it once they receive it

sometimes they don't even understand why they suddenly changed how they feel

they usually struggle trusting other people even when they genuinely care about them

their behavior often comes from both fear of abandonment and fear of getting emotionally hurt

because one part of their brain wants connection while another part expects pain, they often feel confused by their own reactions

What their brain slowly starts believing

"i want someone close, but i'm scared they'll hurt me."

"i don't know if i should trust people."

"love feels safe and dangerous at the same time."

we can describe it in one quote:

"I want you close... but I'm scared of being close."

Fourth: Secure Attachment

people with secure attachment usually feel safe in relationships

they can communicate their feelings without feeling overwhelmed

they enjoy closeness but also respect personal space

they trust people until given a reason not to

their nervous system usually stays calm during normal relationship ups and downs

that doesn't mean they never get hurt

it simply means they don't immediately assume the relationship is ending

they still have disagreements and hard times

the difference is they don't immediately think the relationship is over

What their brain slowly starts believing

"i'm worthy of being loved."

"it's okay to depend on people sometimes."

"problems can be solved without losing each other."

we can say it in one quote:

"I'm loved, and we'll work through this."

Something important

reading this doesn't mean you definitely have one of these attachment styles

people can have traits from more than one style

and attachment styles can change over time

they are learned patterns, not permanent personalities

the goal isn't to put yourself in a box

the goal is to understand yourself a little better

i didn't include every detail because i wanted to keep it simple

if you ever want to understand one attachment style more, you can always search and read more about it

Third : what to do

we are all humens at the end and can get hurt

but this pain doesn't have to ruin our life

we have to remember that these are learned patterns, not our entire personality

what you're experiencing doesn't have to define who you are

these patterns aren't your entire personality

may be ways your brain learned to protect you

your brain learned this pattern over years

that's why it doesn't disappear overnight

healing isn't pretending the fear doesn't exist

it's slowly teaching your brain that not every close relationship ends in pain

it's just a really old wound mark that hasn't fully healed yet

we don't have to let the past control our present or future

at some point we have to decide to start healing

the goal isn't to become a completely different person

the goal is to stop fear from making every decision for you

healing doesn't mean you stop feeling fear

it means you stop letting fear decide everything for you

the first step is understanding yourself

because it's hard to change something if you don't understand what's controlling it

once you understand your patterns you start catching yourself before fear makes the decision for you

little by little your brain starts learning that not everyone will hurt you

that's how healing starts

and one more thing.

healing isn't a straight line.

sometimes you'll feel like you're doing better.

then something happens and you feel like you're back at the beginning.

that doesn't mean you failed.

it just means your brain went back to what it knows.

keep going anyway.

every time you try again, you're teaching your brain something new.

Fourth: why healing is worth it

because imagine living your whole life controlled by fear

pushing away people you love even when you don't want to

missing good memories because your brain keeps expecting the worst

that's not the life any of us deserve

we deserve to enjoy the people we love without being controlled by old fears

Fifth : communication is part of healing

there is one thing i want you to remember

our brain learns from experiences

if it learned fear from painful experiences

it can also learn safety from healthy ones

that's why communication is so important

every time you explain what you're feeling instead of disappearing

your brain learns something new

every time someone understands you instead of hurting you

your brain learns something new

every healthy relationship teaches your brain that not everyone is the same

little by little it starts feeling safer

that's how old patterns slowly begin to change

healing doesn't happen because one day you wake up different

it happens because your brain slowly realizes that it doesn't have to protect you from everyone anymore

that's why, if you can, try not to push away the people who are genuinely trying to understand you

sometimes they are the reason your brain finally learns that it's okay to feel safe again

if your brain learned fear from relationships, it can also learn safety from relationships
